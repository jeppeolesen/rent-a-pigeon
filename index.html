Pro Tips

Get the most out of your feathered operative with these insider pointers.

🧭

Be Specific With Coordinates

Pigeons are smart, but "the guy with the red hat near the park" isn't an address. GPS coordinates or a full street address will dramatically improve delivery accuracy.

🌧️

Check the Weather

Our birds fly in most conditions, but heavy storms can delay even the bravest pigeon. Book 48 hours ahead during rainy season for guaranteed on-time delivery.

✍️

Keep Messages Short

Pigeons carry tiny scrolls, not novels. Aim for 50 words or fewer. If you need more space, upgrade to multi-pigeon relay — it's like pagination but with wings.

🍣

Tip Your Pigeon

A small handful of seeds or breadcrumbs on arrival goes a long way. Well-fed pigeons return for repeat missions with 34% more enthusiasm (source: us).

🎯

Provide a Clear Photo for Poop Strikes

Our birds study their targets carefully. A recent, well-lit photo minimizes collateral damage and ensures the right person gets the message — from above.

👀

Don't Blow the Pigeon's Cover

If you've booked a surveillance op, resist the urge to wave at the bird. Pigeons are professionals, but they can't do their job if you keep pointing at them and grinning.

📅

Book Early for Holidays

Valentine's Day, April Fools, and the week after HOA meetings are our busiest periods. Reserve your pigeon at least a week in advance to avoid disappointment.

🤝

Bundle for Savings

Pair a message delivery with a tactical poop strike and save 15%. We call it the "Sweet & Sour" combo. Your target won't know what hit them — literally.
